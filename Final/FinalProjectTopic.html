Match

Rikki Kendall and Charlotte Roh

Ever needed a study group for a tough course but don’t have any friends? Here is your solution: study group matching. You just register in our database of many other friends also eager to study with you. Create an open chat room for people to join, and you’ll enter in your class name, professor, desired group size, and some information about yourself. Then other people would read through the current existing chat rooms and join your group. Group matching does not have to be exclusive to academics. It could also include getting together for a certain cause, debate about a specific topic, or even hanging out on a lonely Friday night. Sometimes just getting together and talking through different ideas and perspectives helps a lot for understanding topics.

Other uses of the application includes matching you with someone who has taken your course and can help you in return for you to help them in one of their courses. It is like a win-win for both sides and both of you benefit from it. You provide your help and they provide theirs. Sometimes we just need a database that can find these resources for us, especially in such a big community like Duke.

This project would be appropriately sized because it has a similar form to Trello, only the workspace is shared between all users not unique to individuals. They will create a group, or a “list”, that people can join in. Also, this concept is intriguing as it’s something we can use in our everyday lives -- not only simply for our course final project but we can utilize it in the future too.

Possible data sources this project may use are: Firebase for creating user accounts and data storage, javascript for user-input , and Vue.js for UX.
